"The Emperor and the Kite"

"El Emperador y la Cometa"



31. When his sons and daughters heard this, they all fled to a neighboring kingdom where they spent their time sobbing and sighing.



32. But they did nothing else all day long. All except Djeow Seow.



33. She was so tiny, the evil men did not notice her at all.



34. And there she built a hut of twigs and branches.



35. Every day at dawn and again at dark, she would wald across the plain to the tower.



36. And there she would sail her stick-and-paper kite.



37. To the kite string she tied a tiny basket filled with rice and poppyseed cakes, water chestnuts and Green tea.



38. The kite pulled the basket high, high in the air, up as high as the window in the tower.



39. And, in this way, she kept her father alive.



40. So they lived for many days: the emperor in his tower and the princress in a hut near the edge of the plain.
